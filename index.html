How I Get The Job Done

Vision & Strategy

Product vision and strategy are foundational elements of a product's conception. I set strong product visions to ignite passion for the future, and provide purpose to all involved in bringing the product to life. A well-defined strategy helps to establish organizational alignment and focus for effective and efficient product development.

User Research

Users are the core of any product, and I believe researching their needs and problems ensures solutions stay contextual to the user. Combining empathetic, user-centric design with empirical research means we can deliver real value to customers and reduce waste. Adhering to these principles helps us to define and build a product that meets the needs of the business, and delights users.

Process & Prioritization

My product development process focuses on multiple steps, including defining solution-focused feature requirements, crafting meaningful user stories with acceptance criteria, intuitively-designed user flows and data-driven product plans. My skills in prioritization help to determine the time and effort needed to release a product and the rationale behind those decisions. The product roadmap that results is the vehicle that communicates a timeline for delivery, leading to more accurate cost planning.

Refine & Collaborate

Ultimately, products are intended to solve a problem or fulfill a need, and I regularly evaluate whether or not a product is achieving its purpose. I embrace iterative, collaborative techniques that continuously gather feedback from users and internal voices, set strong KPIs that allow for quantitative value measures, and thoughtfully deprecate features. Dual-track agile and lean methodologies lend themselves well to this philosophy.
